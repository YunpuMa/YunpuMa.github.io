I am a Postdoc at the University of Munich, working with Prof. Volker Tresp and Prof. Thomas Seidl. Additionally, I am a research scientist at Siemens, specializing in quantum machine learning for combinatorial optimization problems. Before Siemens, I spent three years as an AI researcher at LMU, where I earned my Ph.D., focusing on temporal knowledge graphs. 


My research interests encompass structured data learning, versatile multi-modal models, and quantum machine learning. I have authored eight primary papers and contributed to 30 co-authored papers in conferences like ICLR, NISP, AAAI, and EMNLP. My ultimate research goal is to advance general AI.


**Markdown generator**

I have also created [a set of Jupyter notebooks](https://github.com/academicpages/academicpages.github.io/tree/master/markdown_generator
) that converts a CSV containing structured data about talks or presentations into individual markdown files that will be properly formatted for the academicpages template. The sample CSVs in that directory are the ones I used to create my own personal website at stuartgeiger.com. My usual workflow is that I keep a spreadsheet of my publications and talks, then run the code in these notebooks to generate the markdown files, then commit and push them to the GitHub repository.
